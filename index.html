Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936 - 2021

Missile Man of Pakistan

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, revered as the "Mohsin-e-Pakistan" (Benefactor of Pakistan), was a towering scientific figure who revolutionized the nation’s defense. By establishing Khan Research Laboratories (KRL), he spearheaded the effort to make Pakistan the first Islamic nuclear power on May 28, 1998. His dedication ensured national sovereignty and regional balance, solidifying his legacy as a national hero and symbol of pride, patriotism, and strength.

Biography

  • Birth: April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, India.
  • Migration: Migrated to Pakistan in 1952, following the partition.
  • Awards: Awarded Nishan-e-Imtiaz and Nishan-e-Quaid-e-Azam, Pakistan’s highest civilian honors.
